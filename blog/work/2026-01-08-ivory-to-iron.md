"Ivory to Iron" or "Ivory to Anvil" is a compact way of saying, "to get things done and make real change in the world, you have to move out of your ivory tower and get to pounding out your iron rods.

It's a reminder, more than anything, that it's easy to remain in design and planning move, but at some point you have to take action.

But there's a catch—someone had to build the ivory tower to begin with: that takes effort, and there is value in taking a high level view of yor surroundings. Planning is essential to product success, but ultimately it is in the working of the steel that results in something tangible and useful to share with the world.
